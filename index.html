Anthony T. Pinter is a PhD Candidate in the Department of Information Science at the University of Colorado Boulder. He works with Jed Brubaker and the Identity Lab exploring how identity changes during life transitions are reflected and represented in online systems such as social media sites. His most recent work focuses these topics in the context of relationship dissolution.

Prior to CU, Anthony completed his B.S. and M.S. in Information Sciences and Technology at the Pennsylvania State University. At Penn State, he worked with Lynette Kvasny Yarger on projects related to the digital divide and discrimination in STEM fields.

In addition to his primary position at CU Boulder as a Graduate Part Time Instructor (GPTI), Anthony concurrently holds the following positions:

  • Vice President of External Affairs for the Graduate and Professional Student Government (GPSG) at CU Boulder
  • Adjunct Part-Time Faculty Instructor for the College of IST through Penn State's World Campus
  • Adjunct Part-Time Faculty Instructor in the Department of Computer Science at St. Bonaventure University
  • Curriculum Coordinator for the iSchool Inclusion Institute (i3)

    In his spare time, Anthony is a prolific music consumer, an avid athlete, and a professional beer drinker.
